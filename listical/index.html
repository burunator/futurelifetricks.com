Tip #1 · Kitchen

Put Salt and Baking Soda Down the Drain Before Bed to Clear a Clogged Sink

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Before you call a plumber, try this. Mix half a cup of coarse salt with a cup of baking soda and pour it straight down the drain right before you go to sleep. Let it sit undisturbed overnight. In the morning, flush with a full kettle of boiling water. The salt acts as a gentle abrasive while the baking soda breaks apart grease and soap buildup. Most minor clogs are gone by breakfast. I've done this at least a dozen times and it has yet to fail me.

Tip #2 · Kitchen

Heat Two Dishes in the Microwave at the Same Time Using a Coffee Mug

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Most microwaves only fit one bowl on the turntable — unless you know this trick. Set one bowl directly on the turntable as normal, then place a wide, sturdy mug beside it. Rest your second bowl on top of the mug. Both dishes rotate and heat at the same time. Add about 25 to 30 extra seconds to account for the larger load. It sounds obvious once you see it, but most people have never thought to try it.

Tip #3 · DIY & Home

Hold a Nail in Place With a Clothespin So You Never Hit Your Fingers With a Hammer

[ Image — 800×500px ]

This is one of those tricks that seems almost too simple — but once you try it, you'll never go back. Grip the nail between the prongs of a wooden clothespin to hold it upright while you line up your swing. Your fingers stay completely clear. When the nail is set far enough into the wall to stand on its own, remove the clothespin and finish hammering normally. Works for small picture hooks, finish nails, and anything else you'd otherwise pinch between your fingers.

Tip #4 · Household

Use a Rotary Can Opener to Cut Through Hard Plastic Packaging Without Getting Cut

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Anyone who has wrestled with clamshell plastic packaging knows how dangerous it can be — the edges are sharp, it twists unpredictably, and scissors often slip right off. The solution is a regular rotary can opener. Place it along the sealed edge of the plastic and turn the handle as you would with a can. It cuts cleanly, stays in control, and eliminates the risk of cutting yourself entirely. Especially useful at the holidays when everything seems to come in the most impenetrable packaging imaginable.

Tip #5 · Organization

Clip Frozen Vegetable Bags Shut and Hang Them From Your Freezer Shelf to Double Your Space

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Most freezers become a jumble of half-open bags within a week. Here's a permanent fix: use binder clips or strong chip bag clips to seal your open bags of frozen vegetables. Then hang them from the wire rack of the shelf so they stand vertically instead of lying flat. You'll free up an enormous amount of floor space, nothing spills when you open the door, and you can actually see what you have. Takes about two minutes to set up and costs next to nothing.

Tip #7 · Cleaning

Remove Soap Scum From Shower Glass in Under Two Minutes With a Dryer Sheet

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Soap scum on glass shower doors is one of those cleaning problems that resists everything — sprays, vinegar, scrubbing. But a slightly dampened dryer sheet (used or new) cuts right through it. The antistatic agents in the sheet break up the mineral and soap buildup on contact. Wipe in small circular motions, then rinse with warm water. The whole door should be clear in about two minutes. It works equally well on chrome fixtures and the inside of the tub.

Tip #8 · Health

Soak Your Feet in Warm Epsom Salt Water Twice a Week to Permanently Eliminate Foot Odor

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Foot odor is caused by bacteria that thrive in warm, moist conditions. Epsom salt — magnesium sulfate — creates an alkaline environment that the bacteria responsible for foot odor simply cannot survive in. Add half a cup to a basin of warm (not scalding) water, soak for 20 minutes, and dry your feet thoroughly afterward, especially between the toes. Do it twice a week for two weeks and the problem is typically gone. Many people who've dealt with this for years are surprised by how quickly and completely it works.

Tip #9 · Money

Freeze Your Credit Card in a Block of Ice to Stop Impulse Spending — It Actually Works

[ Image — 800×500px ]

This sounds like a joke, but it's one of the most effective behavioral finance techniques documented — and it costs nothing to try. Place your credit card in a container of water and put it in the freezer. If you want to make an impulse purchase, you have to wait for the block to thaw before you can use the card. That waiting period — typically several hours — is almost always enough time for the urge to pass. Researchers who study spending behavior call this a "friction strategy." The harder you make it to spend, the less you spend. Simple as that.

Tip #10 · Kitchen

Press a Wet Paper Towel on Top of Leftovers Before Microwaving to Keep Them From Drying Out

[ Image — 800×500px ]

Microwaved food dries out because the heat drives moisture directly out of the food as steam — and that steam just escapes into the air. A damp paper towel placed over your dish traps the steam around the food as it heats. The result is leftovers that come out moist, evenly heated, and close to how they tasted the first time around. Works especially well with rice, pasta, chicken, and pizza. If you've been tolerating dry microwaved leftovers your whole life, this simple change will make a noticeable difference today.
